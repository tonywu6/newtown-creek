Lifelong Learning

Advice to: State Council of the People’s Republic of China

Pitch: The Lifelong Compulsory Education Five-Year Plan (2040)

Under conditions of lifelong learning, it is likely to look more like this: birth, trauma, therapy, training, internship, recruitment, role-playing, training, deployment, trauma, therapy, retraining, deployment, therapy, death.

Currently, we deploy a nine-year compulsory education system, designed in the wake of the opening up policy back in the 1970s. While it has successfully lifted most of our nation out of illiteracy, this antiquated system, based on traditional disciplinary teaching and collective life, is slow, bulky, and cannot be expected to take on the role of preparing our people for a future society of hyper-digitization, artificial-intelligence and automation.

Instead, we propose a lifelong compulsory education system.

  • In this system, schools are abolished. Instead of a collectivized, disciplinary teaching, education tasks are delivered through universally accessible digital devices. This ensures near real-time delivery of the most up-to-date knowledge.
  • In this system, education materials (text “books,” curriculum, knowledge, skills) are no longer designed by publishers/higher-ed institutions, but are developed through iterative artificial intelligence systems. This ensures near real-time update of the most up-to-date knowledge.
  • In this system, the education period is extended indefinitely. This directly addresses the current shortfall, where our schools run independently of our labor market. Instead, education and training become one, where requirements of production are constantly examined and imagined (through A/B tests, blockchain public records, data mining, etc.) and instantly fed back into education.

Work should be understood to be consonant and coterminous with the now perpetually examined life.

With this, citizens become perpetual students of the state. This means that, instead of our current model of fragmented authoritarianism (through stages of central, provincial, local governments), control over social behaviors (and many other aspects such as markets, labor unions, etc.) can be exerted directly by the central government.

Thus lifelong learning operates via a double movement: first, the interiorization, the claiming for the self, of what was previously the work of the state, church, and family; and second, an opening up of possibilities to other kinds of actors and nonstandard learning events.
